Basic

  • Do Great Western Mini-Storage steel buildings need a footer style foundation?

    • This of course depends on the soil conditions of your jobsite and the scope of your storage facility but for most the answer is no. A simple Alaskan Slab (slab on grade foundation) is usually more than adequate.

  • Do we need to have anchor bolts preset in the concrete before we erect the mini-storage?

    • This is another no for most of our mini-storage steel building projects. These buildings do not bear on the foundation like our rigid frame buildings, there load is more disbursed. This allows expansion or epoxy anchors to be drilled into the concrete which saves time and eliminates the possibility of having bolts secured in the concrete out of tolerance.

  • Why are mini-storage unit doors always orange?

    • The orange color for mini-storage roll up doors became popular because of a particular nationwide storage company. Orange was the company color. Many other storage companies seemed to just follow along. In order to provide our customers with the best selection to meet their aesthetic vision Great Western can provide steel roll up doors for you mini-storage building in over a dozen different colors, including the ever popular “sunset orange”.

  • Mini-storage units take a lot of abuse, how difficult is it to get replacement parts?

    • Simple! Great Western can provide milk-run type delivery throughout the lower 48 States. We have numerous vendor partners across the county that can provide replacement panels, doors, fasteners, trims and accessories in the same colors and profiles as your building.

  • Can you design the units to be adjustable in size?

    • Yes and No. If the units are back to back we can design the dividing wall so that it can be moved or removed. We cannot make the units adjustable in width however. Please let your representative know if you would like this feature added to your mini-storage building.
